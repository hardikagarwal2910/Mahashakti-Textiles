02 — PROCESS FLOW

Four steps from yarn to despatch.

PLATE I — THE WARPING CREEL
IOF IV

Warping

Three Jupiter warping machines build beams from the creel — hundreds of ends wound at even tension, the foundation of every fault-free metre.

PLATE II — JUPITER DYEING-SIZING RANGE
IIOF IV

Sizing

Two Jupiter combi ranges size the warp in a single controlled pass, giving each end the strength to survive high-speed insertion.

PLATE III — THE LOOM SHED
IIIOF IV

Weaving

200 Tsudakoma air-jet looms insert the weft on a jet of air — cam looms for speed, dobby looms for structure, up to six weft colours.

PLATE IV — GREIGE ROLLS, PACKED FOR EXPORT
IVOF IV

Singeing, inspection & despatch

Fabric is singed for a clean face, inspected in-house, and packed for export — one gate, one accountable process.

03 — TEXTURISING PLANT

Our looms don't wait for the yarn market.

An in-house texturising plant produces precise-quality polyester yarn for captive consumption. Fabric quality — and delivery dates — stay insulated from the volatility of the open yarn market.

PLATE V — TEXTURISING HALL
